Frequently asked questions

Where is my data stored?

Everything — rosters, games, seasons, your kid's stats — lives on your device only. Hardwoods has no accounts and makes no network requests. See the privacy policy.

What's the difference between the four modes?

Full Scorebook runs the official book: rosters, per-player stats, fouls, periods, and box scores. My Kid tracks a single player from the stands. Simple Scorebook is just the score — two teams, no roster. Team Seasons holds every game you archive from the Full Scorebook: records, trends, and player season averages.

How do I save a game to Team Seasons?

In the Full Scorebook, tap ■ END GAME when the game is over. The box score is archived and the scoreboard resets for your next game — rosters and rules stay put.

How do I share stats?

After a My Kid game, the share card appears automatically — tap Share as Image to text it. You can also share any game or the season from the season view, export a season report PDF, and share the Full Scorebook box score as an image or PDF.

How do seasons work in My Kid?

Games count toward your kid's current season. When a season wraps up, open their profile and tap ⟳ Start New Season — past seasons stay saved and you can flip back to them anytime in the season view.

I recorded something by mistake. How do I fix it?

Tap UNDO — every mode has it, and it tells you exactly what it's undoing. For older mistakes in the Full Scorebook, open the Scorebook, tap ✎ EDIT, then tap any player row to correct their stats directly.

Will my screen turn off during a game?

No — while you're tracking a game, Hardwoods keeps the screen awake. Normal auto-lock resumes when you leave the game screen.

What happens if the app closes mid-game?

Nothing is lost. In-progress games are saved with every tap; reopen the app and pick up where you left off.
